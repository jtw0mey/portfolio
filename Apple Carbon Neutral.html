    P r o j e c t

    Apple have pledged to make their products carbon neutral by 2030. To achieve this goal, they will reduce their 2015 emissions (38.4 million metric tons CO2e) by 75% by 2030. The remaining 25% of gross emissions (9.6 million metric tons CO2e) will be removed using carbon offsets, bringing the net emissions to 0.

    I will be using data provided from Apple's Environmental Progress Reports to visualize their progress towards becoming carbon neutral in 2030.



    F i n d i n g s

    Apple are currently ahead of target in their 2030 goal of reducing their greenhouse gas emissions by 75% to 9m. As of 2022, they have already reduced their emissions by 47% to 20m. At this current rate it is forecasted that Apple will have reduced their greenhouse gas emissions by 86% to 5m by 2030.

    However Apple are currently behind target in their 2030 goal of carbon offsetting 25% of their emissions. As of 2023, Apple had only carbon offsetted 1m of carbon emissions. Although this is currently behind target of offsetting 9m carbon emissions by 2030, Apple can choose to hit this target almost instantly at any moment up until 2030 by investing in environmental projects around the world.

    Apple's carbon footprint by phone can be looked at in two ways. A positive is that Apple have reduced the carbon footprint of their phones by 29% since 2017. However if we look at the full dataset a more negative take arises. Although the carbon footprint has reduced since 2017, the 2023 iPhone still has a bigger carbon footprint than the 2015 iPhone.
