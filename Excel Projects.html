    Regression Analysis

    Microsoft Excel

    P r o j e c t

    Regression analysis is a method of examining the relationship and influence of an independent variable on a dependent variable. For this project I will be using an Excel dataset containing fictional salary data, age and years of experience. I will be using the Analysis ToolPak add-on to run regression on my dataset in Excel, where I want to find out about the relationship between salary and age, and salary and years of experience. This has given me the following results for Salary vs Age:



    We will start by looking at the Regression Statistics. Multiple R  is the correlation coefficient that measures the linear relationship between the variables (salary and age). The value can be any number between -1 and 1:  -1 shows a strong negative relationship, 1 shows a strong positive relationship, and 0 shows there is no relationship. The Salary vs Age has given a Multiple R  correlation coefficient of 0.73, indicating a strong positive relationship. The graph below shows how when one variable goes up, the value of the other tends to do the same.


    R Square  is the coefficient of determination. It measures how many points fall on the regression line as a percentage value, with Salary vs Age having a relatively low R Square value of 53%. This is fairly predictable as regression analysis on some fields of study have an inherently greater amount of unexplainable variation. This data set contains human data about salaries, which generally brings greater variation with studies on human behavior usually producing low scores of less than 50%, as people are hard to predict. This can be seen below with many data points falling far from the regression line.


    Coeffecients  help us build a linear regression equation that will turn our data in to a scatterplot using “Y = bX + a”. Our equation will take shape as “Y = Age Coefficient * x + Intercept”  or “Y = 5041.6 * x - 54136”  as plotted below.







    These are the results for Salary vs Years of Experience:



    The Multiple R  correlation coefficient for Salary vs Years of Experience also displays a strong positive relationship with a value of 0.81, slightly stronger than the Salary vs. Age relationship.


    The R Square  value is again slightly stronger than Salary vs Age with a value of 65%, with more data points falling on the regression line.


    Our Coeffecients  equation will be “Y = Years of Experience Coefficient * x + Intercept”  or “Y = 7039.7 * x - 58384”  to plot our linear regression as seen below.



    Our regression analysis has shown the strong positive relationship with both Salary vs Age and Salary vs Years of Experience, with the latter being slightly stronger. This would make sense as experience is valued higher than age. Experience is one of the main contributors to people earning higher salaries, whereas age alone has little to no effect. The two variables have similar regression lines and relationships with Salary because the more years of experience you have, the higher your age will be, but not vice versa.

    What I Learnt:

    • How to produce regression analysis on an Excel dataset with the Analysis ToolPak add-on

    • How to interpret 'Multiple R' and 'R Square'.

    • How to use coeffecients to create a linear regression equation and scatterplot with regression line.
