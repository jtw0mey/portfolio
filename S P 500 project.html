    P r o j e c t

    I will be using historical stock market data from S&P 500 between 2014 - 2017 to create a dashboard visualising the stock's busiest date, busiest day of the week, and which company has had the most stock traded. I will then take a closer look at Amazon's stock, displaying the stock's volatility and finding out which date was the most volatile.



    F i n d i n g s

    The date with the largest trade volume is 24th August 2015 with 4.6 billion trades taking place on the S&P500 stock market, well above the daily average of 2.1 billion. This was due to one of the biggest 'flash crashes' of recent times, where a very rapid and volatile fall in security prices happen. On 24th August 2015, the S&P500 opened at 1965.15 and dropped by 5% to 1867.01 within minutes. This led to traders panic buying and selling causing the massive trade volume.

    On the 24th August 2015, the stocks that were traded most was Bank of America Corp (BAC) with 215 million trades and Apple (AAPL) with 162 million trades. These two stocks combined made up 8% of the total stock traded on the S&P500 market on this date.

    The day of the week where trade volume is highest is Wednesday with 442 billion trades. All days of the week have a similar level of trade volumes apart from Monday with 374 billion trades. This may be due to traders waiting for more market information after the weekend.

    Amazon (AMZN) stock's volatility was at its highest on 9th June 2017. On this date we see the stock price reach a high of 1013, and a low of 927, a difference of 86. The candestick chart is a good visualization of this volatility with the red bar on this date being the largest on the graph.
