    Tom's Car Showroom

    SQL



    P r o j e c t

    Tom runs a car showroom and his data analyst has just quit leaving him without crucial insights. I plan to analyze his data providing him with answers to the following questions:

    1. What are the details of all cars purchased in the year 2022?
    2. What is the total number of cars sold by each salesperson?
    3. What is the total revenue generated by each salesperson?
    4. What are the details of the cars sold by each salesperson?
    5. What is the total revenue generated by each car type?
    6. What are the details of the cars sold in the year 2021 by salesperson 'Emily Wong'?
    7. What is the total revenue generated by the sales of hatchback cars?
    8. What is the total revenue generated by the sales of SUV cars in the year 2022?
    9. What is the name and city of the salesperson who sold the most number of cars in the year 2023?
    10. What is the name and age of the salesperson who generated the highest revenue in the year 2022?


    D a t a s e t

    Three tables have been created for Sales, Cars and Salespersons. I will be using this data to provide answers to Tom's questions.





    1. What are the details of all cars purchased in the year 2022?


    I selected all the columns from the Cars table. I then used join to combine the columns from the Sales tables with the Cars table using the mutual car_id  column to show all the details for each car purchased. I then used where to specify the cars that were purchased in 2022 only.








    2. What is the total number of cars sold by each salesperson?


    I selected the two columns for salesperson's name and the amount of cars they sold. I calculated the cars_sold column by counting the sale_id's from the Sales table and joining it with the corresponding name from the Salesperson's table using the mutual sale_id  column.








    3. What is the total revenue generated by each salesperson?


    I selected the columns as saleperson's name and total revenue. The total revenue was calculated by using sum to workout each individual's revenue by joining the Sales table data with the car's cost from the Cars table.








    4. What are the details of the cars sold by each salesperson?


    I selected the columns that I wanted to show the details of each car sold by each salesperson. This was done by joining all 3 tables with the mutual salesman_id  and car_id  columns.








    5. What is the total revenue generated by each car type?


    I selected the car's type and its total_revenue as the columns. I then joined the Cars and Sales tables using the car_id  column to be able to produce the total revenue for each car type.








    6. What are the details of the cars sold in the year 2021 by salesperson 'Emily Wong'?


    I selected the columns to show the details of the cars sold by this salesperson. I then joined the Cars and Sales tables using the car_id  column, and I joined Sales and Salespersons tables by joining the salesman_id  columns, to combine details from all 3 tables. I then used where to specify the criteria, which was Emily Wong's sales in the year 2021.








    7. What is the total revenue generated by the sales of hatchback cars?


    I selected the columns to show the total revenue for this style of car. I then joined the Cars and Sales tables using the car_id  column to produce the total revenue by combining the sales volume from the Sales table with the cost from the Cars table.








    8. What is the total revenue generated by the sales of SUV cars in the year 2022?


    I used the same process as the previous question, but I updated the where clause to show only SUV sales in 2022.








    9. What is the name and city of the salesperson who sold the most number of cars in the year 2023?


    I used count to create the cars_sold column. This was done by joining the Salespersons and Sales tables to combine the salesperson's name and city with the amount of cars sold. I then used where to specify that we only wanted 2023 sales.








    10. What is the name and age of the salesperson who generated the highest revenue in the year 2022?


    I used sum to create the total_revenue_2022 column. This was done by joining the Salespersons and Sales tables and the Sales and Cars tables. I then used where to specify that we only wanted 2022 sales.
